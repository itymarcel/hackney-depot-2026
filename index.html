Hackney Depot

Home to makers, artists, designers and thinkers — a community of studios and spaces in the steel-and-glass structure floating above a working London bus depot.

The building

Hackney Depot sits on top of the Ash Grove bus depot, in a remarkable steel-and-glass structure that stood empty for more than 25 years. In 2020 it was brought back to life with a light-touch, eco-friendly retrofit by Turner.Works that celebrates the building’s original character: the glazed roof, cream steel trusses, deep-red ductwork and terracotta studded floors.

Across roughly 2,000 m², the reconfigured floor holds 22 flexible spaces — from intimate private studios to open workspaces, a photo studio and a generous central hall — all filled with daylight and framed by the everyday theatre of the depot below.
